What is Laparoscopy Surgery?

Dr. Jenit Gandhi, recognized as the best laparoscopic surgeon in Ahmedabad, offers advanced laparoscopy surgery at Care And Cure Multispeciality Hospital, Maninagar, Ahmedabad. Laparoscopy is a minimally invasive surgical technique that allows to examine and diagnose issues in the stomach or pelvic area. During the procedure, Dr. Gandhi uses a laparoscope, a thin rod equipped with a video camera, inserted through a small incision in the abdomen, typically half an inch or less. Additional small incisions may be made to insert other surgical instruments, enabling a comprehensive view of the internal organs.

The laparoscope's camera projects real-time images onto a monitor, guiding Dr. Gandhi’s precise hand movements throughout the procedure. Laparoscopy is particularly useful when other diagnostic tests fail to pinpoint the cause of a condition, and it is also employed to collect tissue samples (biopsies) for further testing.

One of the greatest advantages of laparoscopy is that it enables laparoscopy Surgeon to observe the inside of your body without the need for major incisions.

What conditions is laparoscopy used to diagnose?

Laparoscopy is commonly used for a wide range of treatments, including:

  • Gallbladder Removal (Cholecystectomy): Treats gallstones and gallbladder inflammation.
  • Appendectomy: Removes an inflamed appendix, often due to appendicitis.
  • Hernia Surgery: Fixes hernias in the abdominal or groin area with minimal disruption to surrounding tissues.
  • Colorectal Surgery: Removes tumors or diseased sections of the colon and rectum, often for conditions like colorectal cancer or diverticulitis.
  • Bariatric Surgery: Assists in weight loss for patients with obesity, improving overall health and reducing the risk of related conditions.
  • Diagnostic Laparoscopy: A procedure to examine the organs inside the abdomen and diagnose conditions such as unexplained pain or infertility.
  • Laparoscopic Heller’s Myotomy: A surgical procedure to treat achalasia, a condition where the esophagus has trouble moving food into the stomach.
  • Laparoscopic Splenectomy: The removal of the spleen, often due to conditions like spleen enlargement, certain blood disorders, or cancer.

Safe Handling of the Camera :

Dr. Jenit Gandhi ensures the safe handling of the camera during laparoscopy, a critical step in providing clear and precise visualization of the surgical field. The procedure begins by inserting a trocar through a small incision, creating a port for the laparoscope, a thin tube with a camera and light at the end. Dr. Gandhi carefully positions the camera inside the body, allowing him to view magnified images of the internal organs on a monitor, which guides the entire surgical procedure with accuracy and minimizes the risk of injury to surrounding tissues.

Instruments in Laparoscopy:

Dr. Jenit Gandhi utilizes a variety of specialized instruments during laparoscopy to perform surgeries with precision. These include:

  • Trocars: Tubes used to insert other instruments into the body.
  • Graspers (Maryland Dissector): Used for holding and manipulating tissues.
  • Scissors (Metzenbaum Scissors): Designed for precise cutting of tissues.
  • Needle Holders: Used to hold needles for suturing.
  • Suction/Irrigation Devices: To remove fluids and clean the surgical area.
  • Electrocautery Devices: For cutting tissues and sealing blood vessels to prevent bleeding.
